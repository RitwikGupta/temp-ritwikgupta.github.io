#TJRealBeauty, and some flaws

January 11th, 2015 - Ritwik Gupta
A short post

  So don't get me wrong. #TJRealBeauty is a great idea by junior Natalie Miller, and it really does bring to light the issue of body image. However, I feel like the selfies themselves are flawed in a way. No one wants a bad picture of them up on social media. #TJRealBeauty tells people to embrace their body and their flaws by posting an unedited, unfiltered picture up on Instagram. If people really want to embrace themselves, then why are they posing in their selfies? I feel like Renee Miller's #TJRealBeauty post captured the true spirit of the project.

  It's a picture of her straight out of bed. There's no makeup, no posing, no filters. Just a smile. And that's what confidence is about. Personally, I feel like people should embrace that side of themselves, instead of posting a clearly posed selfie.
