Grade Capping

January 20th, 2015 - Ritwik Gupta
A short post

  Now I know you might be thinking, what’s the difference between a kid who got a 50% and one who got a 12%? They’re both failing anyways. So let’s just give them all 50%! Cool, the district average is now higher without any negative effects like grade manufacturing or undeserving people moving on. Just kidding, both things are happening there. In the same vein, we should give people who get a 60% in a class a 98%, because hey, they’re both passing anyways. The jump from 12%-50% is the same as 60%-98%, so no big deal. Wait, we can’t do that? Why not? Why is it wrong to do that now with people who’re actually trying for their grades versus for those who aren’t?

  The fact is, capping grades is grade manufacturing no matter how you portray it. A kid who genuinely doesn’t care reflects the 12% grade, but the 50% might be a kid who tried in class but just didn’t understand. Capping makes them equal. They aren’t equal. Grades are reflective of effort in a class (not necessarily knowledge, but that’s a whole separate issue I have with the GPA system), so it’s not fair to anyone that kids’ grades are being rounded up or down in any manner.

  There’s also the fact that this is fraud under the Department of Education codes, but that’s a whole different issue from the moral issue I’m addressing.

Ritwik Gupta 2015
